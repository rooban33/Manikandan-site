About Me

I am Manigandan Nagarajan, a dedicated research scholar specializing in concrete microstructure and sustainable materials. Currently, I am pursuing my Ph.D. at the College of Engineering Guindy, Anna University, Chennai, where my research focuses on developing low-CO2 cements and investigating supplementary cementitious materials. My doctoral research, titled "Supplementary Cementitious Blend of Metakaolin and Nanosilica with Ferrochrome Slag as Fine Aggregate in Concrete," reflects my commitment to advancing sustainable construction practices. With a strong academic background in Construction Engineering and Management from Sri Ramakrishna Institute of Technology and Civil Engineering from Kalaivani College of Technology, I have consistently pursued innovative projects that aim to reduce the environmental impact of construction materials.


Throughout my career, I have published extensively in high-impact journals and hold patents in the field of sustainable concrete. My professional experience as a Project Engineer at KKM Constructions, along with my teaching roles at Anna University, has enriched my practical and theoretical understanding of the industry. Recognized with multiple awards for my contributions, including Best Paper at ASCE and ACSGE2024 and the International Best Ph.D. Thesis award finalist at IIT Madras RILEM & ICI, I am driven to develop eco-friendly and durable solutions that will shape the future of construction.
